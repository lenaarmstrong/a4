Proposition: Police brutality remains a large problem in the United States.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • (2) Filtered out null values for the number of complaints, year received, and ethnicities
  • (2) Filtered by force since police brutality is defined by excessive force
  • (2) Included groups without complaints
  • (1) Filtered 2010 - 2020 to show recent complaints
  • (1) Choose color by number of civilian complaints

To show how police brutality is still a major problem in the United States, I visualized the number of civilian complaints based on the ethnicity of the person who filed the complaint and the ethnicity of the police officer. First, I filtered out null values for the number of complaints, year received, and ethnicities to remove “null” categories. Then, I filtered the FADO (Force, Abuse of Authority, Discourtesy, and Offensive Language) type by force since police brutality is defined by excessive force and I didn’t want to misrepresent the number of complaints of it. This did decrease the number of complaints against White police officers by Black people from about 5,340 to 1,118, but felt like a more accurate depiction of police violence. If I was trying to be deceptive, I may have included additional categories, but the take-away and trends remain the same, showing how many cases of police brutality occur, especially against Black and Hispanic people by White police officers. I also wanted to answer the question of how police brutality “remains a problem” by filtering by the last ten years in the dataset; however, this number was arbitrarily determined as recent and other choices may have shown slightly different numbers of complaints. I also chose to include groups without complaints because I wanted to make sure more groups were represented by the visualization and not excluded. In this way, it shows that groups that may have been excluded for having fewer complaints are shown as not contributing to this phenomenon as opposed to leaving it up for debate. Additionally, I chose a contrasting color scale from purple to red to better highlight how Black people are disproportionately affected by police brutality. Other color choices, such as a gradient, may have made this less prominent, but I wanted to show the stark differences between the groups and the extent of injustice.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • (2) Filtered out null values of FADO types and year received
  • (-2) Added zero when there was no allegations for a type in a year
  • (-2) Annotated that there were only 4 allegations in 2020 to highlight how few there were
  • (-1) Used a shaded area graph to show the trends between FADO types
  • (-1) Counted "allegations"

In the wake of the 2020 George Floyd protests, democrats and republicans had different ideas about the role of police officers in the United States and concern for the scale of police brutality. I wanted to explore how I could create a counter-visual that would create the sense that police brutality in 2020 was decreasing and not as problematic as some feared to see how data could be warped around different agendas. First, I filtered out allegations that had null values for the FADO type and year received, which did not change the overall visualization and just removed the “null” category, which didn't have additional information. Then, I experimented with more deceptive tactics, such as adding zero when there were no allegations in a particular year for that type. While visualization zero can be very helpful, especially to illuminate what is missing, in this case it visualizes data that wasn’t collected. The data for 2020 only includes allegations in January as opposed to all twelve months like the other years, so by visualizing 0 it suggests that there were no allegations in that year, when there might have been, but just were not collected by the time this dataset was released. Furthermore, but annotating that there are only four allegations in 2020, it emphasizes that there are so few allegations, but deceives the viewer by not mentioning that there were only for January 2020. I used an area graph to further highlight how all the lines decreased and merged in the single digits. Additionally, I used the term "allegations" to refer to the cilivian complaints, even though ProPublica noted that they excluded allegations that were deemed unfounded or to have not occured, to add suspcicion that the numbers in the visualization may be even lower in actuality. The overall effect of this graph is that police violence and abuses decreased, but it obscures socio-political aspects of 2020 and what is the time period of the data.
